Biography

I am a graduate student pursuing a Master's degree in Power Systems Engineering at the University of Tehran, set to finish by August 2024. My academic focus has centered on the dynamic stability of grid-forming inverters, underscored by a notable GPA of 18.21/20 or 3.86/4. Building upon this foundation, I am deeply committed to exploring complex questions within the field and crafting novel solutions. My journey into academia was preceded by a distinguished undergraduate period in Electrical Engineering at the University of Tehran, reaching its peak with the receipt of the Best Undergraduate Project Award in 2022. This developmental period also saw me actively engaged in various research and development endeavors across multiple laboratories and companies, refining my skills in controller design, simulation, and experimental implementation. With a blend of theoretical knowledge and practical experience, I am poised to contribute significantly to the discussion surrounding power systems and power electronic engineering, particularly in the domain of inverters' stability and control, and DC-DC converters.
